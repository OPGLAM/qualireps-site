# Diamond Push-Up
**Push horizontal · reps · bilateral**
*The last bilateral tier. Triceps lockout and the compact press — elbows close to the body — that the one-arm push-up demands.*

## The movement
From a plank, hands together under the sternum with index fingers and thumbs touching to form a diamond, lower until the lower chest meets the hands, then press back to a clean lockout. The narrow hand position forces the triceps to do more and keeps the press tight to the ribs.

## Set-up — and what each part is for
- **Hands** together under the sternum, index fingers and thumbs touching. This is what loads the triceps and forces the elbows to stay tucked.
- **Feet** on the balls of the feet, heels lifted, hip-width.
- **Body** one straight line. Glutes squeezed, abdominals braced, ribs down. → *Feel the front panel hard; the lower back stays neutral.*
- **Shoulders** down, packed; serratus active under the armpit.
- **Neck** neutral, eyes slightly ahead.

## The rep — rehearse it before you do it
- **Down (1–2 s).** Elbows skim the ribs — no bell-shaped flare. The lower chest descends to meet the hands. → *Feel the triceps and the inner chest take the load, with the lat catching the descent at the side of the trunk.*
- **Bottom.** Lower chest at the hands, brief still pause. → *Feel the shoulders set, not strained; the trunk has not softened.*
- **Up.** Press back to a complete lockout. Shoulders stay packed down, not shrugging toward the ears.

## Watch for
- **Elbows flaring out** to cheat the load → keep them grazing the ribs.
- **Lower back arching** as the trunk softens → re-brace before each descent.
- **Range cut short at the bottom** → the chest must reach the hands.
- **Shoulders shrugging up** at lockout → keep them packed down.

## Breathing
Brace at the top, hold through the descent, pressurized exhale through the lockout.

## Within the level
- *Harder:* 3 s descent; pause at the bottom; feet together to tighten the line.
- *Easier:* hands a touch farther apart — keep the elbows tucked.

## Dose
Test a clean max; work at half (rounded down), max 5 reps per set, spread through the day. Stop each set well short of failure.
